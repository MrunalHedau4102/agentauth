# Code of Conduct

## Our Commitment

We are committed to providing a welcoming and inspiring community for all. We pledge that everyone participating in the AgentAuth project and its communities will treat each other with respect and contribute to open, welcoming, and inclusive conversations.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- **Being respectful** — Disagreement doesn't excuse bad manners
- **Being inclusive** — Welcome diverse viewpoints and backgrounds
- **Being constructive** — Disagree respectfully, offer solutions
- **Being professional** — No harassment, discrimination, or offensive content
- **Being genuine** — Be authentic and honest in your interactions

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- Harassment, discrimination, or intimidation
- Offensive comments related to personal characteristics
- Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
- Public or private attacks
- Publishing others' private information
- Illegal conduct
- Any form of abuse

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- **All project spaces** — GitHub issues, PRs, discussions, code
- **Public spaces** — Conferences, meetups, online communities
- **Private channels** — Direct messages, Slack, Discord
- **Enforcement** — Applies to all community members and maintainers

## Reporting Violations

If you experience or witness a violation of this code of conduct:

1. **Report privately** — Email conduct@authlib.dev with details
2. **Include context** — Share what happened, who was involved, when
3. **Stay safe** — Please prioritize your wellbeing

We will:
- Acknowledge receipt within 48 hours
- Investigate promptly
- Handle reports confidentially
- Take appropriate action

## Consequences

Violations may result in:

- **Warning** — For minor or first-time violations
- **Temporary Ban** — For repeated violations (7-30 days)
- **Permanent Ban** — For severe or persistent violations
- **Legal Action** — For criminal behavior

## Appeals

Persons receiving enforcement action may appeal by emailing conduct@authlib.dev within 30 days with:

- Detailed explanation
- Evidence of change
- Commitment to improvement

An appeal committee will review within 7 days.
